Part of me is thinking I should give up on trying to make a compliant mechanism piano action and just make the bloody keyboard glockenspiel
with an ordinary action.

But part of me finds the puzzle irresistible, so I think I am going to persist.

I also would prefer more like 10x lever ratio instead of only 3x.

Meh, forget it, it is too springy and vibratey and wobbly. I'm going to forget the whole compliant mechanism boondoggle and just design an

What kind of lever ratio would a typical piano action have?

On this one: https://img.incoherency.co.uk/6513

It actually looks like the ratio is only about 2x? The distance between the hammer and the string is only 2x the key travel.

I found an alternative diagram where it is 2.5x. So is it more that it's highly non-linear? Rather than being an overall high ratio. If the
first part of the key travel gives very little hammer movement, and then the last part gives a lot, you effectively have a higher lever
ratio to accelerate the hammer at the end?

According to ChatGPT a typical ratio is more like 5x or 6x, defined as "hammer travel to let-off / key travel to let-off". What is let-off?

I think my error is that I measured it as though the felt key stop didn't exist. Looking again: https://img.incoherency.co.uk/6514

So that's almost 4x now instead of 2x. And this has 6.4x: https://img.incoherency.co.uk/6515

I guess aim for 6x overall.
